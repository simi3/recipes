Moong daal with coriander
As the weather has been so rainy recently we’ve been making lots of warming dishes and this coconut and lentil dahl has been my favourite. Cooking the lentils in coconut milk makes each bite much creamier, while the plum tomatoes, cumin, paprika, cinnamon, curry powder and cayenne pepper add such a lovely blend of flavours.

I love this served with rice, natural (coconut) yoghurt and avocado slices, and sprinkled with chopped coriander and chilli flakes.

  • MAKES
    Serves 4
  • LEVEL
    easy
  • PREP TIME
    20 minutes
  • COOKING TIME
    4 hours

Ingredients

FOR THE DAHL
  • 400g of dry green lentils, 2 cups
  • 2 x 400 ml cans of coconut milk
  • 3 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp of ground cumin
  • 1 tsp of cinnamon
  • 1 tsp of paprika
  • 1 tsp of chilli powder
  • 1 tsp of curry powder
  • Handful of chilli flakes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
TO SERVE
  • A serving of rice
  • Sliced avocado
  • Chilli flakes
  • Dollop of coconut yogurt

Method

1. Prepare ingredients

Thinly slice 1 onion. Crush or finely chop the garlic. Peel and finely grate the ginger. Trim and cut the lamb into 1.5 cm chunks.

2. Cook aromatics

Heat 1 tbs oil in a large deep frypan over medium heat. Add the garam masala, onion, garlic, ginger and ¼ tsp salt, and cook, stirring, for 3-5 mins until slightly softened and fragrant.

3. Cook lamb

Increase heat to high, add the lamb and stir for 2 mins or until starting to brown. Add the water (see staples list), cover with a lid and reduce heat to low. Cook for 15 mins or until the lamb is tender.

4. Prepare broccoli 'rice'

Meanwhile, using a food processor, knife or box grater, finely chop or coarsely grate the broccoli until it resembles coarse breadcrumbs. Pick the mint leaves, discarding the stems, and tear any larger leaves, reserving a few leaves to serve.

5. Cook broccoli 'rice'

Heat 2 tsp oil in a large frypan and cook broccoli for 1-2 mins until lightly cooked. Season with salt. Cover and keep warm. Combine half the chopped mint with the yoghurt and season with salt.
